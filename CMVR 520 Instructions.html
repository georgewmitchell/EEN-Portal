Chassis
Remove EN-CH-101 chassis from cardboard box and store cardboard box and packing materials for later use. 
Put all hardware aside except blue SATA cables, case rails and motherboard standoffs
Put hardware box to the side.
Remove enclosure handles


Remove lid


Find Chassis Standoffs and line up the motherboard to the chassis


Line up Zotac MB-H87I-AE Motherboard with chassis and install (2) standoffs closest to the rear of the motherboard.
Remove adhesive backing on chassis mat and stick to chassis.
Install the standoffs in the lined up position


Screw standoffs into the chassis (2)


Remove stock motherboard backplate and replace with EN-BP-XXX add pics


Remove USB Card expansion plate from front of chassis. Ther is a tab in back of the tray that releases this. 

Pull the drive bay tray out to access the ports





Disconnect the cables for the USB/Serial Port



Remove the SuperMicro logo from front of the chassis. Replace label with a 520 decal

Apply a 5" Eagle Eye Label to the top of chassis (PIC)
Apply a CAM WAN Label to backplate

Insert memory in slot closest to CPU. Slot labled DDRIII1 (PIC)

Connect Cables to motherboard.
Connect ATX 12v Power cable to ATX 12v slot on motherboard. There are 2 of these but only connect one and it doesn't matter which one. (PIC)
Connect ATX cable to ATX slot on motherboard (PIC)
Disconnect Existing F-Panel connector which is found at the top of the drive bay which is furthest to the right. (PIC)
Connect EN-CB-102 Front panel split cable to this same port. Push in this cable very tight.
Connect other end EN-CB-102 cables to motherboard
Use the Zotac MB-H87I-AE F-Panel pin configuration from the manual and plug the following cables into the motherboard. Note that the negative pole is always the black wires with this cable.

Power SW
Plug this in with the label facing away from the cpu


Reset SW
Plug this in with the label facing away from the cpu

Power LED aka Message/Sleep LED
Move the green pin next to the black one. This is a 3pin connector. Plug this in with the label facing away from the CPU This connector is a 3 pin. Move the green pin cable to the middle of the connector and plug this in with the label facing away from the cpu

HDD LED
Plug this in with the label facing away from the cpu

Fan Spiltter Cable
Connect EN-CB-103 Fan splitter cable to CPU fan connection on motherboard. Plug (4) fans into this cable
Connect 4 pin white power supply cable into Fan splitter cable
Connect other 4 pin white power supply cable into Hard Drive power card that is right behind drive bay #1;
Connect black connector with green & white wire to the port on motherboard that is labeled sys fan PIC
Make sure all cables are clear from fans

Install Hard Drives
4 Hard drive ports are numerically oriented from left to right. Hard drive number 1 is the left most drive bay if the case is facing you.
Connect the white Hard Drive Power cable to the white hard drive card connector.
Label the cables 1-4
Bay 1 gets the 2nd longest blue sata cable. The cable will be facing down and the 90 deg end is plugged into the HD Card PIC
Bay 2 gets the 3rd longest blue sata cable PIC
Bay 3 gets a left angle EN-CB-104 Cable. Remove the fans strip out of the way to plug this in. The 90 deg side gets plugged into the Hard Drive cardPIC
Bay 4 gets the longest blue sata cable and is routed through the foam insert. The 90 deg side gets plugged into the motherboardPIC

Unwrap (4) 4TB Western Digital HD
Remove 4 Hard drive sleeves from chassis
Remove 2 screws from tan sleeve inserts
Mount Hard Drive in sleeve using (4) screws. The drive connectors should be facing towards the back of the sleeve. Do this for all 4 drive bays.
Insert the 4 assembled drives into all 4 bays
Using the Zotac MB-H87I-AE Motherboard Layout Identify the Sata Connector configuration and plug the corresponding drive cables to the right Sata ports on the motherboard

Install Lid  (2 screws)
Slide lid into place and lock into tabs
Attach back of lid with 2 screws

Connect external cables
Connect Power, Ethernet, monitor, keyboard to the bridge

Install software on bridge. Refer to software instructions
Print and instruction sheet with Attach ID # and place unit on the "Processed" shelf
